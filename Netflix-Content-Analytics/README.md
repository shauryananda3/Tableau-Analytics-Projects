# 🎬 Netflix Content Analytics

A professional Tableau analytics project designed to evaluate Netflix’s global content library across Movies, TV Shows, ratings, genres, countries, and release trends.

This dashboard helps streaming strategists, content planners, and analysts understand content mix, audience targeting, market expansion opportunities, and platform growth trends using data-driven insights.

---

# 📌 Business Objective

Streaming platforms need visibility into content distribution, genre demand, ratings mix, and regional expansion trends to optimize content investment and subscriber growth.

This dashboard enables stakeholders to:

- Analyze Movies vs TV Shows distribution  
- Monitor rating-based audience targeting  
- Compare country-wise content availability  
- Identify top-performing genres  
- Evaluate yearly content growth trends  
- Support strategic content planning using analytics

# 🔍 Key Insights

## 🎥 Content Mix Insights

- **Movies dominate the Netflix library** with **4,265 titles (68.42%)**.  
- **TV Shows account for 1,969 titles (31.58%)**, indicating a movie-heavy platform strategy.  
- The platform historically prioritized faster-to-scale movie content over long-series production.

## 📺 Ratings Insights

- **TV-MA** is the largest category with **2,027 titles**, showing strong focus on mature audiences.  
- **TV-14** follows with **1,698 titles**, indicating broad teen/adult targeting.  
- Family-friendly categories such as **PG, TV-PG, Kids TV** are comparatively smaller.

## 🌍 Country Insights

- Content concentration appears strongest in **North America, India, and parts of Europe**.  
- India shows notable content presence, reflecting strategic international growth.  
- Emerging markets appear underpenetrated relative to global opportunity.

## 🎭 Genre Insights

- **Documentaries** rank as the #1 genre (**299 titles**).  
- **Stand-Up Comedy** ranks second (**273 titles**).  
- International drama/comedy combinations are strongly represented.  
- Genre mix suggests Netflix balances low-cost scalable content with global appeal formats.

## 📈 Growth Trend Insights

- Content additions accelerated sharply from **2016 onward**.  
- Peak expansion occurred during **2018–2019**.  
- Movies drove most of the growth surge.  
- 2020 drop likely reflects incomplete data / pandemic production impact.

# 💡 Recommendations

## Content Strategy

- Increase premium TV Show investment to balance heavy movie dependency.  
- Expand successful documentary and stand-up comedy formats due to proven scale.  
- Develop more binge-worthy serialized content for retention.

## Audience Strategy

- Continue mature audience offerings but increase family/kids content share.  
- Build targeted teen catalog around TV-14 demand.  
- Introduce regional family programming in emerging markets.

## Global Expansion

- Increase local-language originals in underrepresented regions.  
- Replicate India-style localization strategy across Southeast Asia, LATAM, and Africa.  
- Use country demand data for region-specific genre investments.

## Portfolio Optimization

- Reduce oversaturation in weaker-performing movie genres.  
- Increase international dramas and hybrid genres with global traction.  
- Maintain balanced pipeline between low-cost quick wins and flagship originals.

## Growth & Retention

- Use TV Shows for subscriber retention and Movies for acquisition.  
- Build franchise ecosystems around successful genres.  
- Time major releases quarterly to smooth subscriber churn.
